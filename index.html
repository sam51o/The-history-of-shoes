The history of shoes

Section 1

The earliest known shoes are sagebrush bark sandals dating from approximately 7000 or 8000 BC, found in the Fort Rock Cave in the US state of Oregon in 1938.

Section 2

These boat-shaped flat open shoes are made from woven reed and most likely the oldest surviving pair in the V&A collection. The straps are also made of long, thin reed, covered in a strip of a wider reed. The construction of these practical overshoes continued in the same style into the 19th century.
